## Artefact 1 – Australia’s AI Ethics Principles
**Source:** Australian Government – Australia's AI Ethics Principles
![Australia's AI Ethics Principles](images/ai-ethics.png.png)
The Australian Government has released its principles for the ethical use of AI. This document aims to provide guidelines on the responsible design, creation and application of AI technologies. The Australian AI ethics principles provide the concept of accountability, of AI systems being transparent, fair, trustworthy, safe and accountable, and of the need to respect human rights and privacy requirements. Additionally, the principles come up with a number of suggestions on how to become aware of social issues related to AI and win public support of the ethical use of AI technologies.

This article is what's made me pick it because it seems that making AI means thinking about the impact that AI can have on humans and society, along with the development of the technology. I have to admit that after listening to Workshop 2 on ethics in AI in this class I have realized that the principles of fairness, transparency and accountability are among the key principles of reliable AI technologies.

## Artefact 3 – Generative AI in Education
**Source:** OpenAI – ChatGPT
![Generative AI in Education](images/generative-ai.png.png)
Summary

The learning experience is being transformed by Generative AI, which is changing the way students and educators work, in areas such as creating material, utilizing feedback, and conducting research. The advantages of tools such as ChatGPT in education stem from their ability to facilitate explanation of ideas, provide answers to a variety of questions, and aid in writing itself. Nonetheless, Generative AI raises certain concerns, such as those of integrity in academics, misinformation, privacy, and too much reliance on AI-generated material. Thus, the usage of Generative AI must be judicious and involve finding ethical solutions.

Reflection
The reason I chose this artifact is that Generative AI is gaining importance in everyday life nowadays, and it is relevant because it pertains to my studies of Information Technology. Moreover, I learned from Workshop 2 that AI can only be useful when utilized in an ethical and responsible manner while dismissing unethical ways of employing the technology.
